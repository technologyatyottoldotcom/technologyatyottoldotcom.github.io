Executive Summary

Crabel Multi-Product 1x is a highly diversified portfolio of uncorrelated, predominantly short-term systematic trading strategies across ~250 global futures and FX markets. Founded in 1987 by Toby Crabel, a pioneer in short-term price pattern research, the firm manages $4.92bn across multiple programs. The Multi-Product flagship ($1.39bn strategy AUM) targets 8% annualised volatility and has delivered a 7.59% CAGR net of fees since March 1998 — a 28-year live track record.

Key Strengths

28-year live track record with 7.59% CAGR, near-zero equity correlation (0.06 to S&P 500), genuine portfolio diversifier.
Exceptional institutional infrastructure: Deloitte audit, BNY Mellon custody, 7 tier-1 counterparties, 60 staff.
Highly liquid: monthly redemptions, 5-day notice, no lock-up. ~250 markets traded with ~1-day avg holding period.

Key Risks

Recent performance weakness: -6.24% in 2025, 5Y CAGR only 2.42% vs 7.59% SI — alpha erosion concerns.
Expensive fee structure: 2/30 quarterly — among the highest in systematic/CTA space.
Key-man dependency on Toby Crabel (founder/CIO), though mitigated by co-PM structure (Pomada, Wisdom).

Strategy & Edge

Return Driver: Systematic capture of persistent short-term price anomalies (momentum and mean-reversion) across ~250 global futures and FX markets. Strategies are evidence-based, symmetric (long/short), and predominantly held 1-2 days. Volatility allocation: Momentum 45%, Mean Reversion 30%, Trend Participation 12%, Unique 13%.

Edge: Technological and analytical. Proprietary low-latency infrastructure with global co-location (Asia, NA, Europe), proprietary execution algorithms, and a 30-year research library of short-term price patterns. Machine learning layer for factor recombination. Edge is structural — execution speed and research depth are difficult to replicate.

Process: Sector allocation targets: Equities 41%, Fixed Income 23%, Commodities 21%, FX 15%. Risk controlled via position-level stops, time exits, VaR/co-variance capping, dynamic portfolio sizing, and a 2.5% intraday max-loss stop. All trades symmetrical long/short.
