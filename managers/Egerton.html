Executive Summary

Egerton Capital is one of London's most respected long/short equity managers, founded by John Armitage in 1994. The strategy runs a quality-focused European/global L/S equity book with high net exposure typical (60-80% net long). Concentrated 25-40 position long book with diversified shorts. Strong fundamental research culture with multi-decade tenure across the team. AUM ~$25bn.

Key Strengths

30+ year track record under same CIO (John Armitage); among Europe's most respected fundamental investors.
Strong long-term compounding: outperformed MSCI Europe by ~5% p.a. since inception.
Quality bias has produced consistent fundamentals; lower drawdowns than European indices.
Deep European research network and corporate access.
Stable team and AUM base — institutional credibility.

Key Risks

High net exposure (60-80%) = meaningful market beta.
European focus has been a structural headwind vs US in 2020s.
Armitage is 65+; succession planning in progress.
Concentrated longs create idiosyncratic risk.
Short alpha has been challenged in recent quality-momentum regime.
