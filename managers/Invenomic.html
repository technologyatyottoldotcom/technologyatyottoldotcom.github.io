Executive Summary

Invenomic Capital Management is a Boston-based long/short equity manager founded by Ali Motamed, formerly Co-PM of Boston Partners Long/Short Equity and Morningstar's 2014 Alternatives PM of the Year. The strategy runs a value-and-quality tilted US equity L/S book with variable net exposure (40-60% typical, range 0-80%). Very diversified: 143 longs, 108 shorts as of Feb 2026. Strong track record since Sep 2021: +35% in 2022, +14% in 2023, -9% in 2024 (value drawdown), +6% Feb 2026 suggests recovery. Strategy AUM $2.47bn.

Key Strengths

PM Ali Motamed has deep L/S credentials (Morningstar PM of the Year 2014 at Boston Partners).
Highly diversified (143 longs / 108 shorts) — low single-name fragility.
Value + quality + forensic accounting lens — differentiated from momentum/growth peers.
Strong in 2022 inflation regime (+35.3%), demonstrating style diversification value.
Attractive fee structure — 1.0%/20% on institutional class.
Daily liquidity on UCITS vehicle; monthly on offshore.

Key Risks

2024 was weak (-8.5%) as value/small-cap underperformed mega-cap momentum.
High gross exposure (194% recent) means meaningful leverage.
Short book has large-cap bias (-80% large) — short pain in mega-cap rally regimes.
Smaller firm than peers; key-person risk on Ali Motamed.
Relatively short track record at Invenomic (since 2021).

Strategy, Edge & Process

Return Driver: Single-stock alpha in US equities, long and short. Long book targets value-and-quality with margin of safety; short book targets weak fundamentals, stretched valuations, accounting red flags, structural disruption.

Edge: Motamed's quality-of-earnings and forensic accounting lens, honed at Boston Partners. Variable net via bottom-up selection, not market timing. High diversification minimizes blow-up risk.

Construction: Target net 40-60% (range 0-80%). Gross 130-200% (recent 194%). 100-150 longs, 80-120 shorts. Large-cap shorts (-80%); balanced longs.

Vehicle & Terms: Offshore Cayman master-feeder + Irish UCITS feeder. Daily UCITS / monthly offshore. 1.0/20 institutional, 1.5/20 retail, all HWM.

Risk Management: Position-level sizing, sector exposure monitoring, stop-loss discipline, short-book liquidity management.
