Executive Summary

Sirios Capital Management is a Boston-based long/short equity manager founded in 1999, with John F. Brennan Jr. serving as CIO and lead PM. The strategy invests predominantly in US large-cap equities (minimum 51% US) using a fundamental bottom-up research process. Variable net exposure (44-54% recent) and benchmark-unconstrained — objective is to outperform broad indices over a cycle with lower volatility.

Key Strengths

25+ year track record under same CIO (John Brennan); strong fundamental research culture.
Flexible net exposure management — has reduced net into stressed markets historically.
Focus on both long and short alpha generation; diversified across sectors.
Reasonable capacity with institutional infrastructure.

Key Risks

Recent performance has lagged — fund flagged as style-challenged in mega-cap momentum tape.
Short book has been a persistent drag in post-2020 US large-cap momentum regime.
Net long bias (40-60%) implies meaningful directional beta in equity drawdowns.
Modest AUM relative to peers — capacity for institutional tickets but not enormous.

Strategy, Edge & Process

Return Driver: Fundamental single-stock alpha in US large-cap equities. Long book research-driven on durable franchises and reasonable valuations; short book on structurally-challenged businesses and accounting red flags.

Edge: Deep US research coverage; cycle-tested PM (Tech bust, GFC, COVID); willingness to lower net exposure when valuations extend.

Construction: 40-80 longs, 30-60 shorts. Net 30-60% typical. Gross 150-180%. US-focused with selective Europe/Asia.

Vehicle & Terms: Cayman master-feeder. Monthly subs / quarterly redemptions. 1.5/20 with HWM.

Risk Management: Position-level sizing, sector concentration caps, daily VaR.
