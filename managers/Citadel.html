Executive Summary

Citadel's flagship multi-strategy fund, managed by Ken Griffin since 1990. Wellington deploys five core strategies — commodities, equities, fixed income, credit, and quantitative — through a centralized but multi-PM architecture. AUM ~$67bn (post $5bn profit return Jan 2026). Annualized return of 19% since inception (35 years). Ranked as the most profitable hedge fund since inception with $88bn+ in cumulative net gains. 2024: +15.1%, 2025: +10.2%. All five strategies positive in both years. The fund returned $32bn in profits to investors since 2017.

Key Strengths

35-year track record; 19% annualized net since 1990 — among the best long-term records in the industry.
Multi-strategy diversification across five uncorrelated alpha engines eliminates single-strategy fragility.
$67bn AUM with capacity discipline — returned $5bn in 2025 profits to cap assets.
Griffin alignment: principals own ~20% ($9bn) of Wellington, ensuring incentive alignment.
Citadel Securities (market-making arm) provides informational edge and technology infrastructure.

Key Risks

Very high fees: 2/20 + passthrough costs — total expense can exceed 5-6% in strong years.
Black-box multi-strategy — LPs have limited transparency into individual pod/strategy P&L.
Key-man risk: Ken Griffin is the central figure; succession is a long-term concern despite deep bench.
Capacity: at $67bn, alpha generation per dollar deployed may compress over time.
Regulatory/political visibility increasing as firm grows.

Strategy, Edge & Process

Return Driver: Alpha from five diversified strategy engines: equities (fundamental L/S), fixed income & rates, credit, commodities, and quantitative/systematic. Each run by specialist PMs under centralized risk.

Edge: Scale-driven infrastructure advantage; Citadel Securities technology and data feeds; 35 years of compounded intellectual capital; ability to attract top talent with compensation and infrastructure unmatched by smaller shops.

Construction: Multi-PM pods within each strategy bucket. Centralized risk management aggregates exposures across all pods. Gross leverage varies by strategy but is substantial at the aggregate level.

Vehicle & Terms: Cayman master-feeder. Quarterly redemptions with notice. 2/20 + passthrough expenses. High minimums. USD base class.

Risk Management: Real-time centralized risk across all pods; factor exposure monitoring; VaR limits; stop-loss at pod level.
