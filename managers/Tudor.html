Executive Summary

Paul Tudor Jones's flagship fund, founded 1980. One of the original macro legends. AUM ~$22bn (BVI portfolio). The fund has evolved from pure discretionary macro to a hybrid multi-strategy approach. Recent performance has been mixed — the firm has cut fees on various funds in acknowledgment of challenging returns relative to multi-strat peers. Tudor Jones remains actively involved but the firm is increasingly team-managed.

Key Strengths

Paul Tudor Jones: legendary macro trader; 45-year career.
Hybrid macro + multi-strategy evolution provides diversification.
$22bn AUM; institutional infrastructure.
Tudor Jones's alignment (significant personal capital).

Key Risks

Performance has been inconsistent in recent years.
Fee cuts signal competitive pressure.
Macro style faces same headwinds as Caxton/Moore.
Tudor Jones is 71; succession planning relevant.
